Foundations Reflection

Three things I learnt about myself and my ego during core learning:

  • I am relatively humble most of the time
  • I still need to learn how to apply all of these core skills into my day to day life
  • I don't think I am that egotistical from what I have seen so far


Self-awareness plays a big role in learning and coding as it lets you know what your limitations are. If you have no self-awareness, it might mean you can be over estimating yourself; you could start struggling with problems that you thought were easy but you don't actually know how to properly solve them.

The most surprising thing about core learning is that a lot of it is stuff that I have learnt previously in other areas of study or even just by myself on the internet. It is stuff that you come across every day but you never really think about. The most challenging aspects of core learning was writing the blogs since its been a long time since I've properly written essay style blogs, it has mostly just been reports of data anaylsis in the past few years. It was a good experience relearning how to write in a blog and essay style again.

I think Devacademy focuses on core learning a lot since it helps people learn every day skills that are very useful at work or even at home. Solidifying these skills means that you can be a better programmer at work and work with others in teams in a more efficient way. I don't think it is a waste of time to learn these core skills; practising programming can obviously make you a better programmer but learning core skills can make you a better person.
