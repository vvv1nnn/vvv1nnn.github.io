Identity, Values and Strengths

Ethics, the moral principles that govern behaviour, serve as the compass guiding individuals and societies towards what is deemed right and just. Rooted in philosophies, cultures, and religions, ethics shape our decisions, interactions, and societal structures. They provide a framework for distinguishing between right and wrong, fair and unfair, moral and immoral. Ethical considerations encompass a wide array of topics, from individual actions to global policies, encompassing issues such as justice, integrity, honesty, compassion, and respect for others. Upholding ethical standards fosters trust, integrity, and harmony within communities, while ethical lapses can lead to distrust, conflict, and societal breakdown.


My personal belief in ethics and a value that I hold highly is to live by the rule of “in the happiness of others, lies our own”. This phrase was widely used by His Holiness Pramukh Swami Maharaj, a spiritual leader of BAPS which is a sect of Hinduism. It was a way of life for him and I try to abide by the same way of life. I can think of many instances where I personally have sacrificed part of my own happiness to make others happy, even if it was something insignificant like giving the last lollipop to a small child rather than keeping it for yourself or giving a plushy away to a baby that was crying at the arcade instead of keeping it. I think there is immense gratification in making others happy and expecting no return from it.

I think this ties into the fact that I am an INFP on the Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI); this personality type has the introverted, intuitive, feeling and prospective personality traits. 16personalities.com states that “INFP personalities share a sincere curiosity about the depths of human nature. Introspective to the core, they're exquisitely attuned to their own thoughts and feelings, but they yearn to understand the people around them as well.” INFPs tend to be empathetic, generous, open-minded, creative, passionate and idealistic. I think this will help me on my learning journey as when I'm working with others I'm more accepting of others ideas and see things from others point of view easier; I also strive to follow my conscience and rarely lose sight of my goals so that also helps with doing projects. Some weaknesses include being unrealistic, self-isolating, unfocused at times, emotionally vulnerable, too eager to please others and too self critical. I think some of these can hinder my learning process as sometimes I try to be too perfect; one example would be when creating the blog website, I tried making everything as perfect as possible and went through 3 iterations of designs before I chose one I actually liked and that ate up a lot of time that I could've spent on doing something else.


When I was a project engineer in construction, I used to lead a team of around 10 or more people as a very young engineer. This meant most of the labourers wouldn't have much respect for me as I was young and they wouldn't listen to what I say. I had to prove myself and show that I knew what I was talking about for the teams I was in to respect me. Sometimes there was internal conflict and I had to solve it; one instance was when I had organised the weekly plan and structure and some of the team members were unhappy with the schedule. I had to compromise at the time and change it up to how they thought it should be as I wanted to keep my team happy and also take into consideration what their thoughts were in the plan. I think I would do the same now unless their inputs into the schedule were flawed and would mess things up.
